The Taliban Are Letting These Girls Go to School in Afghanistan

The Taliban have not publicized the fact that they have allowed some schools in the north to stay open

Text by Margherita Stancati, Reporting and Photos by Elise Blanchard
Aug. 13, 2022

After the Taliban returned to power in August 2021, they shut down schools for girls over 6th grade, effectively barring women from getting an education.

The decision to keep schools shut for older girls has drawn widespread international condemnation, and has been an obstacle to Afghanistan receiving desperately-needed financial assistance.

But not all schools are closed, reflecting a divide within the Taliban on this issue. In parts of northern Afghanistan--where women have typically had more active roles in society than much of the rest of the country--some girls' schools have reopened and others never really shut.

The Taliban have not publicized the fact that they have allowed some schools in the north to stay open, like this one in Mazar-i-Sharif and did not want to be named when expressing their views.
